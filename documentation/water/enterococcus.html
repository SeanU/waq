Enterococcus

What is it?

A bacterial subset of Fecal Coliforms that normally live in the intestinal tracts of warm-blooded animals, including humans.

Where does it come from?

Enterocuccus indicates possible contamination of streams and rivers by fecal waste.

Health effects

Enterococci are typically not considered harmful to humans, but has shown a greater correlation with swimming-associated gastrointestinal illness in both marine and fresh waters than other bacterial organisms.
