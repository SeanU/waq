Know More About WAQ

WAQ is a toxic substance data consolidation platform which allows virtually any user easy access to their overall exposure levels of pollutants while providing the the ability to drill down into specifics with context, resources, and analysis seamlessly integrated into visualizations. The WAQ platform empowers users with digestible and accessible information to make pertinent decisions for themselves and their families. This way the next time you take your kid out for a long hike and let your dog swim in the stream you are aware of your exposure.

WAQ uses raw air and water quality data from the US Evironmental Protection Agency (EPA) as well as EPA regulation levels and standards to provide the user with digestable information on contaminants thruoghout the US. The data is cleaned, standardized, and consolidated to provide easy access to any user.

The main WAQ page allows user to search for any location natiowide and quickly see nearby measurment sites and the status of polluants on a color coded scale.

    Red samples are above regulated limits and may pose health risks
    Yellow samples are below regulated limits but above recomended levels and prolonged exposure may pose risk
    Green samples are below recomended limits and pose no health risks

WAQ also provides a machine learning model estimate of the location searched, which is predicted using historical data. This estimate allows the user to see a prediction for areas that may not have as many sample sites nearby. The model estimate is differentied from the actual measurements to distunguish concrete measurements from estimataions.
