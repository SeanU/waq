E. coli

What is it?

E. coli is a type of fecal coliform bacteria commonly found in the intestines of animals and humans. E. coli is short for Escherichia coli.

Where does it come from?

The presence of E. coli in water is a strong indication of recent sewage or animal waste contamination.

Health effects

Most strains are harmless and live in the intestines of healthy humans and animals, E. coli O157:H7 is one strain that produces a powerful toxin and can cause severe illness. Infection often causes severe bloody diarrhea and abdominal cramps; sometimes the infection causes non-bloody diarrhea. In some people, particularly children under 5 years of age and the elderly, the infection can also cause additional complications.
